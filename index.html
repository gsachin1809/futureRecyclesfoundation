About Us

Not-For-Profit organisation working in the city of Mumbai to create awareness among citizens.

Future Recyclers Foundation is an 80-G registered not-for-profit organisation working in the city of Mumbai to create awareness among citizens about different types of waste & explore sustainable ways to manage waste. We conduct workshops like

  • Art & Climate Change
  • Zero-Waste Cooking
  • Basics of Segregation
  • Home & Community Composting for low-income communities & schools

so that we can change the mindset of people from waste management to resource management. Future Recyclers Foundation provides tailor-made sustainable solutions to many residential, commercial, hotels & educational institutes for efficient onsite waste management. Future Recyclers Foundation is the retail partner of Daily Dump, India’s first home-based composter of urban spaces offer path-breaking solutions for waste management at the level of homes, offices, and public spaces, without the intervention of government agencies.

Our Work

Projects

Community Composting in Housing societies like Veena Dynasty Vasai-Virar (350 flats), Aashish Co-op Ghatkopar (65 flats), Sangita Co-op Hsg Society (52 flats). Our various community initiatives have helped every 100 flats to divert around 7 tonnes of organic resources (through the kitchen waste like fruit peels, residual seeds, and skin) from the landfill every year. It has helped societies make significant strides in waste management thus reducing dependency on the BMC and other organizations deployed for waste disposal around the city.

Workshops

Art & Climate Change (low-income schools), Zero-Waste Cooking (home-based chefs from low-income communities), Basics of Segregation, Home & Community Composting (for housing societies & bulk waste generators), DIY Natural Cleansers, and many more. These workshops have received an enormous response and have helped a lot of homemakers, students, and other attendees to make their homemade cleansers. We have also contributed by supporting and lending a hand to initiatives and projects which tend to support society and contribute to making a significant change in the livelihood of people.

What is Composting?

Composting is a natural way to recycle decomposed organic materials. Composting is a natural process; it happens to all organic matter. Our bodies will decompose, trees decompose, food decomposes, paper decomposes, wood decomposes, and anything that was once a living thing decomposes. That is nature’s method of making sure there is no waste.

Benefits Of Compost

Composting has several significant benefits. It can help the environment by reducing landfill waste and erosion. It keeps your lawn and garden healthy by controlling weeds and keeping pests at bay. It can even help to conserve resources and save you money!

Prevents Soil Erosion

Compost helps to reduce soil erosion in a number of ways, including by binding soil together, increasing infiltration, and slowing the surface flow of water.

Assists in Stormwater Management

Compost helps to control water flows on and through soil, thereby proving to be a capable tool for stormwater management

Benefits Of Compost

Composting has several significant benefits. It can help the environment by reducing landfill waste and erosion. It keeps your lawn and garden healthy by controlling weeds and keeping pests at bay. It can even help to conserve resources and save you money!

Prevents Soil Erosion

Promotes Healthier Plant Growth

Compost is best known and used for its promotion of healthy plant growth and suppression of plant disease.

Assists in Stormwater Management

Compost helps to control water flows on and through soil, thereby proving to be a capable tool for stormwater management

Conserves Water

In addition to helping to manage stormwater, compost can also conserve water due to its ability to retain and efficiently transfer water. This both helps the environment and makes your project more drought resistant.

Reduces Waste

Waste reduction is another benefit of compost, since compost is generally made from waste (food scraps, organic byproducts, etc) that is diverted from landfill flows. This reduces the amount of waste going to landfills and it upcycles those materials into a productive, environmentally beneficial product.

Combats Climate Change

Compost is also a potent tool in combating climate change. It cuts down on greenhouse gases entering the atmosphere and makes ecosystems more resilient to rising temperatures. .

Reduces Project Maintenance Costs

Compost can also lower long-term maintenance costs for projects. This is because compost promotes healthy plant growth, thereby reducing mortality and subsequent replacement costs. In addition, compost promotes drought resistance which lowers artificial irrigation costs. Learn how compost can lower project maintenance costs in these and other ways.

Improves Soil Health

By adding nutrients and soil biota, compost improves the biological, chemical and structural health of soils. This further contributes to healthy plants and successful projects.

Assists in Wetland Reclamation

Compost can assist State DOTs in activities related to the protection, reclamation, or creation of wetlands. Compost laid down between roadways and wetlands can filter pollutants caused by automobile traffic. In addition, compost is a great additive to wetland reclamation or projects since those ecosystems require highly organic material.
